Index

Accumsan
From Latin "accumulare", meaning accumulation or buildup, often used to describe a buildup of pressure or growth in a system.
Condimentum
Latin for seasoning or spice; here metaphorically refers to added interest or variety.
Facilisis
Derived from Latin "facilis", meaning easy; refers to ease or smoothness of a process.
Gravida
Latin for heavy or pregnant; in medical or poetic usage, refers to weight or importance.
Lacinia
Means fringe or border; in anatomy, can mean a lacy part of tissue; metaphorically here for complexity or detail.
Pellentesque
From Latin "pellere", meaning to drive or push; used in CSS frameworks but here just filler Latin.
Phasellus
Latin for a type of bean plant; commonly used as a placeholder word in Lorem Ipsum text.
Ultricies
From Latin "ultricies", meaning beyond or extreme; implies high intensity or strength.
Vehicula
From Latin "vehiculum", meaning vehicle; refers metaphorically to a medium of transport or movement.
